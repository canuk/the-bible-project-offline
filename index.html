Wisdom Series

What does it mean to live well in God's good world? Proverbs, Ecclesiastes and Job comprise the Bible's wisdom literature, and they join together to answer that question from three very distinct points of view.


Proverbs

This fully animated video is the first in our Wisdom Series that dives into three books (Proverbs, Ecclesiastes, and Job) that provide scriptural advice for how to live well in the world. Using the metaphor of the "personality" of each book, we imagine Proverbs as an enlightened teacher-one who speaks of khockmah (wisdom) and the essence of God, Lady Wisdom, who guides us to use khockmah to create a blessed life.

Gospel Series

The Gospels in the New Testament tell the one story of Jesus from four distinct points of view. This fully animated series explores the unique literary design and key themes of each Gospel account.


Luke

See how Jesus’ announcement of good news for the poor began with the circumstances of his birth. His entry into the world brought Israel’s ancient hopes to their fulfillment, and also began to turn the values of our world order upside-down.

Watch Video »


Mark

John Mark, master story-teller, gives us this Gospel addressing the question: Is Jesus the Jewish Messiah? Detailing the life of Jesus, Mark highlights the tension between the Jew's hopes about what the conquering Messiah would do (overthrow the Romans), and the reality of what Jesus came to do (be sacrificed by the Romans). Addressing the "Who" of Jesus in the first part of the book, and and the "How" of what He came to do in the third, Mark offers insight into the conflicting expectations that God's people had for their coming King. What did Jesus say to these conditions? How did He respond to His followers?

Word Study Series

Explore important Greek and Hebrew words that are foundational for understanding the biblical story. We'll unpack the original meaning of these words and show how they illuminate our understanding of the world still today.


Shema - "Listen"

The first installment of our new Word Studies series is an exploration of the ancient biblical prayer called “The Shema” (found in Deuteronomy 6:4-6). For thousands of years Jewish people have daily prayed these words which summarize the Bible’s call for faithfulness and devotion to God. We will explore all of the key words in this prayer and what they meant in their original language and historical context.
